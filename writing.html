Themes

Recurring questions

What makes liberty durable?

Not merely the absence of command, but the presence of institutions, habits, and civic forms through which people can coordinate without domination.

How do markets coordinate knowledge?

Markets are not mechanical allocation devices. They are dynamic discovery processes embedded in law, property, prices, and expectations.

Why does law matter for development?

Economic freedom depends on rules that ordinary people can use: contract, property, tort, dispute resolution, and procedural fairness.

What does self-government require?

Self-government is learned through participation, claim-making, local responsibility, associational life, and a language of civic action.
